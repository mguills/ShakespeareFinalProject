 HIERONIMO: Long may live serve Sovereign Liege, And soon decay, unless serve Liege. HIERONIMO: That son, gracious sovereign, Of whom, though tender infancy My loving thoughts never hope well, He never pleased father's eyes til now, Nor filled heart over-cloying joys. ... HIERONIMO: But I know Grace wise, And might seem partial difference, Enforced nature law arms My tongue plead young Horatio's right. He hunted well lion's death, ... Not garment wore skin; So Hares may pull dead lions beard. HIERONIMO: The first armed knight, hung Scutcheon up, Was English Robert, Earl Gloucester, Who, King Stephen bore sway Albion, Arrived five twenty thousand men In Portingale, success war Enforced King, Saracen, ... To bear yoke English monarchy. HIERONIMO: The second knight hung Scutcheon up, Was Edmund, Earl Kent Albion, When English Richard wore diadem. He came likewise razed Lisbon walls And took King Portingale fight; ... For such-like service done, He created Duke York. HIERONIMO: The third last, least account Was, rest, valiant Englishman, Brave John Gaunt, Duke Lancaster, As Scutcheon plainly may appear. ... He puissant army came Spain, And took King Castile prisoner.
